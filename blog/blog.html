How Goose Took Flight

      Goose (pictured above) is one of the latest jam bands to cross the music scene. Starting small, spawning from a band named Orthelo, Goose rose to the top playing with names such as Trey Anastasio from the band Phish.

      The first time I heard Goose, fireworks appeared. I'm not kidding, it was the fourth of July and I saw literal fireworks as "Arcadia" played on the speaker. I immediately ran to find out who I was hearing on guitar. Rick Mitarotonda is who I was hearing. From there I was hooked. Rick's melody lines mixed with percussive rhythm guitar shown, but then I heard the sounds of piano shining through. Playing that piano was Peter Anspach. Also a guitarist, Peter played down the foundation of Arcadia with full and rich piano chords that ran through unique guitar pedals for a cool and modern effect. That was my sudden introduction to Goose.

      The next day I listened to no one else but them. The song that stood out that day was "So Ready". The star in this track, bass player Trevor Weekz. His hook in So Ready was stuck in my head for weeks, bouncing around not wanting to come out (and I was fine with that). A deeper dive into more of their live tracks relieved a gold mine of bass licks and rhythms that went on forever. His likeness to the greats like Phil Lesh from the Grateful Dead is prominent. Then lastly there are the drummers. To be specific, drummer and percussionist. Jeff Arevalo and Ben Atkind drive this rhythm section with a firm grip on the wheel. You can hear the classic jam band rhythms, but these two bring out the best in each other which leads to funky fills that make you rewind and re-listen.

      If this does not make you want to listen, then do yourself a favor and put them on anyway. As anyone knows, words cannot describe magic, and magic is what Goose is.
